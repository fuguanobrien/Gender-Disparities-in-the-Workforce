# Gender Disparities in the Workforce
Data Source: [National Survey of College Graduates 2023](https://ncses.nsf.gov/surveys/national-survey-college-graduates/2023)

This project investigates how **gender, education level, and age** influence salary differences among full-time workers in the United States. The goal is to better understand whether higher education reduces the gender pay gap and how the gap evolves over a worker’s career. 

### Research Questions

* Does the effect of **gender on salary** vary depending on **education level** and **age**?
* Does having an **advanced degree** reduce the gender pay gap?
* Does the **gender pay gap increase with age** among highly educated workers? 

### Dataset and Methodology

The analysis focuses on **U.S. full-time workers (40+ hours per week)** and examines the following variables:

* **Dependent Variable:** Salary (USD)
* **Independent Variables:** Gender, Age, Education Level
* **Control Variable:** Presence of children

Education was grouped into:

* **Bachelor’s Degree**
* **Advanced Degree**

Age was categorized into:

* **Younger (≤40)**
* **Older (>40)** 

Statistical methods include **descriptive analysis and regression models** to evaluate gender differences in salary across education and age groups.

### Key Findings

#### 1. Basic Gender Pay Gap

Men consistently earn higher salaries than women. Salary distributions show men are more represented in higher salary ranges while women’s earnings cluster more in middle and lower ranges. 

#### 2. Hypothesis 1: Education and the Gender Gap

**Hypothesis:** Advanced degrees reduce the gender pay gap.

**Result:** Not supported.
The salary gap was actually **larger among workers with advanced degrees** than among those with bachelor’s degrees, and regression results showed the difference was **not statistically significant**. 

#### 3. Hypothesis 2: Age and the Gender Gap

**Hypothesis:** The gender pay gap increases with age among workers with advanced degrees.

**Result:** Supported.
Older employees with advanced degrees experience a **significantly larger gender pay gap** than younger employees. Regression analysis showed the male wage advantage increases by about **3.3% per age group**, indicating widening disparities over time. 

### Key Takeaways

* Higher education **raises salaries but does not eliminate gender pay disparities**.
* The gender pay gap **widens over the course of a career**, especially among highly educated workers.
* Structural and organizational factors likely contribute to **cumulative disadvantages for women over time**. 

### Limitations

* Winsorized salary data may **underestimate pay disparities**, especially among top earners.
* Differences in **hours worked** were not fully controlled.
* The dataset focuses only on **U.S. full-time workers**, limiting generalizability.
